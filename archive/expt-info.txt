================================================================================Wound_Respirometry_2023 - README================================================================================PROJECT INFORMATION-------------------Title: Wound_Respirometry_2023Principal Investigator: Adrian Stier (UCSB), Ashley Seifert (UKY),                         Craig Osenberg (UGA)Collaborators: Ninah Munk, Kelsey Vaughn, Lily Zhao, Josh SarliProject Dates: May Ð June 2023Contact: Adrian Stier - astier@ucsb.eduRESEARCH OBJECTIVES-------------------This project investigates how coral colony metabolism responds to tissue injuries of different sizes. By measuring whole-colony respirometry, we aim to assess the energetic costs of injury and wound healing. The study aims to:- Quantify changes in coral colony metabolic rates following small and large   tissue injuries.- Determine whether injury size differentially impacts coral oxygen consumption   and production.- Assess whether metabolic rate recovery differs between coral species with   different skeletal structures.- Standardize respirometry methods for future studies.DATA SOURCES & COLLECTION METHODS----------------------------------Experimental Design:Respirometry Setup: A 10-chamber system was used to measure oxygen consumption, with nine chambers for coral samples and one blank chamber to measure background seawater oxygen levels.Coral Species Studied:  - Porites (perforate species)  - Acropora pulchra (imperforate species)Sample Size: 18 colonies per species (n=36 total corals).Treatment Groups:Group           Sample Size    Wound Type-----------     -----------    ------------------------------------------Unwounded       6/species      No injurySmall Wound     6/species      8 mm branch cut (Acropora) or                                ?" circular wound (Porites)Large Wound     6/species      8 mm second branch cut (Acropora) or                                ?" circular wound (Porites)DATA ORGANIZATION & ACCESSIBILITY----------------------------------Folder Structure:/5.
